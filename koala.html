KOALA

The koala (Phascolarctos cinereus) is an arboreal herbivorous marsupial native to Australia. It is the only extant representative of the family Phascolarctidae and its closest living relatives are the wombats.

Koalas are known for their teddy bear-like appearance, with a stout, tailless body and large head with round, fluffy ears. They have sharp claws for climbing and a specialized digestive system that allows them to eat toxic eucalyptus leaves.

Koalas sleep about 20 hours a day due to their low-energy diet. They are mostly active at night and spend most of their waking hours feeding. Despite their cute appearance, koalas have a loud, deep growl and can be aggressive when disturbed.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

Our koala habitat is located in the Australian Outback exhibit (Pen B07), featuring eucalyptus trees and a climate-controlled environment to keep them comfortable year-round.
