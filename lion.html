LION

The lion (Panthera leo) is a large cat of the genus Panthera, native to Africa and India. It has a muscular, deep-chested body, short, rounded head, round ears, and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail. Adult male lions are larger than females and have a prominent mane.

Lions inhabit grasslands, savannas, and shrublands. They are more social than other big cats, living in groups called prides. A lion's pride consists of a few adult males, related females, and cubs. Females typically do the hunting, while males patrol the territory and protect the pride.

Lions are apex predators and keystone predators, meaning they play a crucial role in their ecosystem. Their prey consists mainly of mammals, particularly ungulates weighing 190-550 kg (420-1,210 lb).
