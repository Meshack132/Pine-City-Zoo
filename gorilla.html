MOUNTAIN GORILLA

The mountain gorilla (Gorilla beringei beringei) is one of the two subspecies of the eastern gorilla. These magnificent primates are the largest living primates, with males reaching up to 1.8 m (5 ft 11 in) in height and weighing up to 220 kg (485 lb).

Gorillas live in stable family groups led by a dominant silverback male. They are primarily herbivorous, feeding on leaves, shoots, stems, and occasionally fruit. Despite their size and strength, gorillas are generally gentle and shy.

These intelligent animals use tools in the wild and have been observed displaying human-like emotions. They communicate through vocalizations, body postures, and facial expressions. Each gorilla has a unique nose print, similar to human fingerprints.
